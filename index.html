Our Strategy

Simply put, we charge a flat 1% fee on all buys, and use at least half of that cost to reinvest to the pool.

Through the Facade

The sad truth is that most crypto application is jargon. If a crypto claims something like governance or web applications, outside of an ecosystem that the crypto project itself made, it's completely useless. Most staking rewards are unsustainable because either the token is non mintable, so the staking will run out, or too high of yeilds from staking will heavily inflate the supply and lower the cost of tokens. Visa and Mastercard are actually much better at handling high transaction volume, for example Visa regularly handles over a million transactions per minute where ethereum can maybe hit a thousand. ðŸ˜Ÿ That's Bad!
Solana is the only blockchain that can handle volumes close to Visa, and is cheap, but credit systems are currenlty setup to have no cost to the spender, which will always be a better system for increasing spending volume. Crypto is made for decentralized investing unsurprisingly that's the one area that Crypto outshines everything else.

How Crypto Value Grows

The only thing that keeps any crypto or asset relevant is people buying it. Purchases are also how crypto projects make money, whenever a buy order is put in, the Liquidity Provider(LP), person who setup the crypto, receives money from the fees charged. This can be anywhere from 0.2% of the total transaction to almost 10% of the total buy volume! For example, when you buy $100 worth of crypto, you'll be charged a fee anywhere from a few cents to a couple dollars, the LP gets most of the money you're charged in fees.(Likely between half to three quarters of the fee)
Most of the money LP get goes to costs. For example if a crypto has staking, they have to pay a fee every time anyone receives any staking rewards. This cost adds up very quickely and requires massive pools of money to keep going, when buy orders slow down, that pool drains and staking rewards lower, which will happen over time.
Everything for Solana Dinero has no maintenance costs, so when we receive LP profits we can use them to reinvest to the pool in order to raise the price and market cap over time. After a certain point of profit, 100% of our LP profits will be used for reinvestment to raise the pool value over time. In the $100 example, we would charge $1 in fees, then create an additional buy order of 50 cents that will never get sold from the pool. As long as people don't sell more than buy, and Solana's price doesn't go down too much, this should raise the value of SOLDN long term.

Break Down

  1. Charge 1% fee on buy transaction
  2. Use half of that fee to reinvest
  3. Do nothing else, keep cost low

Work Smarter not Harder! We do nearly nothing, on purpose. Money doesn't have to be complicated, and investments just need people buying them. We have no staff, overhead, loans to repay, no smart contracts to tie up funds, just growth.
