Our Strategy

Simply put, Solana Dinero ($SOLDN) charges a flat 1% fee on all buys, and uses over half of that fee to reinvest to the pool, raising it's price floor.

Through the Facade

First, we must address the sad truth about crypto, most application completely overstated. Most crytpo with application only has value if its ecosystem takes off, and outside of Proof of Stake blockchains, like Solana, staking is unsustainable or inflationary. Payment processors like Visa and Mastercard are much better at handling high transaction volume, for example Visa regularly handles over a million transactions per minute where Ethereum barely hits a thousand. ðŸ˜Ÿ That's Bad! Solana is the only blockchain that can handle volumes close to Visa, and is cheap, but payment systems are currenlty setup to have no cost to the spender. Crypto tokens are made for decentralized and low cost investing unsurprisingly that's the one area that Crypto outshines most other things. However, any cryptos value is still entirely up to the amount of people buying it.


How Crypto Value Grows

Like any asset, the only thing that keeps a Crypto relevant is people buying it. Purchases are also how crypto projects make money, whenever someone buys it, the Liquidity Provider(LP), a.k.a the person who setup the crypto pool, receives money from the fees charged. This can be anywhere from 0.2% of the total transaction to almost 10% of the total buy volume! For example, when you buy $100 worth of crypto, you'll be charged a fee anywhere from a few cents to a couple dollars, the LP gets most of that money.(Likely between 50% to 75% of the fee)
In most Crypto projects, LP profits go partially to costs, or in meme projects like on pump.fun, the entire LP reward gets taken as profit. For example if a crypto has staking, they have to pay for the smart contract and a fee every time anyone receives any rewards. This cost adds up very quickly and requires massive pools of money to keep going. When buy orders slow down, that pool drains and staking rewards lower, which will always happen over time.


Solana Dinero's Simple Plan

Everything for Solana Dinero ($SOLDN) has been setup to have no maintenance costs. When we receive LP profits we can use them to reinvest to the pool in order to raise the price floor over time. This will consistently raise the minimum value that SOLDN can drop to, so over time, this will create growth (Although we can't guarantee everyone will profit). If we exceed $100k in profit annually, 100% of our LP profits that year will be used for reinvestment to the pool.
In the $100 Example, the Dex will charge $1 in fees, we receive a little over half of that dollar and put in a buy order for 50 ¢. This 50 ¢ remains in the pool forever, as long and Solana's price doesn't go down too much, this should raise the value of $SOLDN long term.


Break Down

  1. Charge 1% fee on buy transaction
  2. Use half of that fee to reinvest
  3. Do nothing else, incur no costs

Work Smarter not Harder! We do nearly nothing, on purpose. Money doesn't have to be complicated, and investments just need people buying them. We have no staff, no overhead, no loans to repay, and no smart contracts to tie up funds, just growth!
