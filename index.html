Our Strategy

Simply put, Solana Dinero ($SOLDN) charges a flat 1% fee on all buys, and uses over half of that cost to reinvest to the pool.

Through the Facade

First lets address the sad truth, most crypto application is jargon. If a crypto claims something like governance, web applications, or even in game assets, outside of their ecosystem, they're completely useless. The only thing in any circumstance that gives something value, is people wanting to buy it. Most staking rewards are unsustainable because either the token is non mintable, so the staking will run out, or too high of yeilds from staking will heavily inflate the supply and lower the cost of tokens. Staking long term is primarily for Proof of Stake blockchainsm like Solana.
For handling money transactions, Visa and Mastercard are actually much better at handling the necessary high volume. For example Visa regularly handles over a million transactions per minute where Ethereum barely hits a thousand. ðŸ˜Ÿ That's Bad! Solana is the only blockchain that can handle volumes close to Visa, and is cheap, but credit systems are currenlty setup to have no cost to the spender, which will always be a better system for increasing spending volume. Crypto tokens are made for decentralized investing unsurprisingly that's the one area that Crypto outshines everything else. However, it's value is still entirely up to people buying it.


How Crypto Value Grows

The only thing that keeps any crypto or asset relevant is people buying it. Purchases are also how crypto projects make money, whenever a buy order is put in, the Liquidity Provider(LP), person who setup the crypto, receives money from the fees charged. This can be anywhere from 0.2% of the total transaction to almost 10% of the total buy volume! For example, when you buy $100 worth of crypto, you'll be charged a fee anywhere from a few cents to a couple dollars, the LP gets most of that money.(Likely between 1/2 to 3/4 of the fee)
Most of the money LP get goes to costs. For example if a crypto has staking, they have to pay for the smart contract and a fee every time anyone receives any rewards. This cost adds up very quickly and requires massive pools of money to keep going. When buy orders slow down, that pool drains and staking rewards lower, which will happen over time.
Everything for Solana Dinero has no maintenance costs, so when we receive LP profits we can use them to reinvest to the pool in order to raise the price and market cap over time. After a certain point of profit, 100% of our LP profits will be used for reinvestment to raise the pool value over time.
In the $100 example, the Dex charges $1 in fees, we receive a little over half of that dollar and put in a buy order for 50 cents. This 50 ¢ remains in the pool forever, and as long and Solana's price doesn't go down too much, this should raise the value of $SOLDN long term.

  3. Do nothing else, incur no costs
